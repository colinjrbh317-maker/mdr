## Common Roof Repair Problems in Virginia

Virginia's climate doesn't go easy on roofs. Between the humid summers, heavy spring rains, ice storms in winter, and the occasional hailstorm rolling through the New River Valley, your roof takes a beating year-round. Here are the issues we see most often on service calls in the Christiansburg and Roanoke area.

### Wind Damage and Missing Shingles

High winds peel back shingle tabs, break the seal strip, and sometimes rip shingles clean off. Even if you don't see shingles in the yard, wind can lift tabs enough to break the waterproof bond underneath. Once that seal is broken, water has a path in.

### Ice Dams and Winter Damage

When snow melts and refreezes at the eaves, ice dams form and push water backwards under the shingles. We see this every winter in the higher elevations around Blacksburg and the surrounding mountains. The damage often doesn't show up until spring.

### Hail Damage

Hail doesn't always punch holes through your roof. More often, it bruises the shingles — knocking loose the protective granules and exposing the asphalt mat underneath. That accelerates aging and leads to leaks down the road. After a hailstorm, you might not see obvious damage from the ground, but up close it tells a different story.

### Flashing Failures

The metal flashing around chimneys, skylights, vent pipes, and wall intersections is one of the most common leak sources we find. Flashing corrodes, pulls away from surfaces, or gets sealed with generic caulk that fails within a couple of years. A surprising number of our repair calls come down to flashing that was installed incorrectly or patched with the wrong sealant.

### Valley Leaks

Roof valleys channel a lot of water, and if the valley flashing is damaged, improperly overlapped, or clogged with debris, leaks follow. We see this frequently on older homes around Roanoke where original valley metal has corroded or shifted over time.

### Tree Damage

Overhanging branches scrape shingles and drop debris that traps moisture. Falling limbs during storms cause obvious damage, but even small branches landing on the roof can crack shingles or dislodge flashing. Trees close to the house are one of the most underestimated threats to roof longevity in this area.

## How We Diagnose Roof Leaks — Finding the Real Source

Here's something most homeowners don't realize: the spot where water shows up inside your house is almost never directly below the actual leak. ## Roof Repair vs. Roof Replacement — How to Decide

Not every damaged roof needs to be torn off and replaced. But not every damaged roof should be patched, either. Here's the honest breakdown we give homeowners every day:

**Repair makes sense when:**
- Damage is localized to one area or a few shingles
- Your roof is less than 15 years old and otherwise in good shape
- The decking underneath is solid
- You have one layer of shingles (not multiple layers)

**Replacement makes more sense when:**
- Damage is widespread across multiple slopes
- Shingles are curling, cracking, or losing granules everywhere
- You're seeing recurring leaks in different spots
- The roof is approaching or past its expected lifespan (20+ years for standard architectural shingles)
- Decking has soft spots or rot in multiple areas

We'll never push a replacement when a repair will hold. And we'll never patch a roof that really needs to come off — because that just delays the inevitable and costs you more in the long run.

## Quality Materials and Proper Repair Techniques

A repair is only as good as the materials and methods behind it. Here's what sets our work apart:

**Matching existing shingles.** We don't slap on whatever shingle is cheapest at the supply house. We match the color, profile, and manufacturer line as closely as possible so your repair blends in — not sticks out.

**Proper flashing installation.** We use step flashing, counter flashing, and kick-out flashing where needed. We don't rely on caulk or roof cement as a primary waterproofing method. Those are temporary patches, not real repairs.

**Full system approach.** When we replace shingles, we also check and replace the underlayment, ice-and-water shield (in vulnerable areas), and any damaged decking. Putting new shingles over compromised materials underneath is just covering up the problem.

## Warning Signs You Need Roof Repair Now

Don't wait on these. If you see any of the following, call us sooner rather than later:

- **Active water leaks** — Water stains growing on ceilings or walls, dripping, or pooling in the attic
- **Sagging roof deck** — Visible dip or sag in the roofline, which can indicate structural damage
- **Daylight through the roof** — If you can see light from the attic, water can get in too
- **Shingles in the yard** — Missing shingles after a storm mean your roof is exposed
- **Granule buildup in gutters** — A sign shingles are deteriorating and losing their protective surface
- **Damaged or missing flashing** — Exposed gaps around chimneys, vents, or walls
- **Mold or mildew in the attic** — Often signals ongoing moisture intrusion you haven't noticed yet

Catching problems early keeps repair costs down and prevents small issues from turning into major headaches.
