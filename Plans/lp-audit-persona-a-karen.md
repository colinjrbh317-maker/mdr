# Persona A — Karen (Urgent Roof Leak, Roanoke, 55)

## Scorecard
- Clarity: 8/10 (form card says "Book" but button says "Continue" — language gap)
- Trust: 7/10 (anti-upsell copy is strongest line; 231 reviews unverifiable; needs real quote in hero)
- Visual: 7/10 (form-first mobile is aggressive — asked for info before she knows us)
- Mobile friction: 5/10 (2-step form + SMS consent wall + tiny legal text)
- CTA strength: 6/10 (3 different CTA phrasings: "Get My Free Inspection", "Continue →", "Get My Free Quote")
- Would submit form: 4/10 (more likely to tap sticky call bar)

## Top 5 issues

**1. SMS consent wall = conversion killer.** 55-word legal paragraph in text-xs, REQUIRED to proceed. Button disabled until checked. For a skeptical 55-year-old in panic mode, this is a hard stop.
- Fix: Move SMS consent to step 2 as optional checkbox. Step 1 = Name + Phone only.

**2. 2-step form fatigue.** Step 2 has email, address, service dropdown, textarea. Cognitive overload in a panic.
- Fix: Pre-select "Roof Repair" from campaign slug. Drop textarea on repair LP. Rename "Skip & submit" → "Submit without details".

**3. Unverifiable "231 reviews" + "zero unpaid warranty claims".** No tap-to-Google link. No pull quote in hero. Self-reported warranty stat reads as BS.
- Fix: Real review pull quote in hero + tappable Google link on "231 reviews".

**4. Form card says "Book", button says "Continue", final says "Get My Free Quote".** Three phrasings for same action.
- Fix: Standardize to "Get Same-Day Diagnosis" (repair LP) across card title + both buttons.

**5. "We Call Within 24 Hours" contradicts "Same-day diagnosis" promise.** 3-step trust section undercuts urgency.
- Fix: Change to "We call back within the hour (often immediately)".

## What Karen would love
- "Is this an emergency?" radio at top of form → routes to emergency queue
- Real Roanoke review quote IN hero (not buried below)
- "Most repairs $300–$900" price callout near form
- Photo of actual crew/van (not just drone)
- Visible VA contractor license number (DPOR-issued)

## Trust-killers
- "Zero unpaid warranty claims" (unverifiable)
- SMS consent wall gating the form
- "Continue →" button mismatching hero CTA

## Mobile friction
- Form above headline (sees form before knowing who we are)
- text-xs legal copy (Karen is 55)
- Sticky call bar eats 56px of form field tap zone
- "Skip & submit" link tap target < 44px
- 15+ sections from hero form to final CTA — nobody scrolls that far in an emergency

## Verdict
Karen taps sticky call bar, bypasses form. Copy earns the tap. 2-step form + SMS wall + "Continue" ambiguity + no proof near form = she calls instead of submitting.
