## Part 2: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Who MDR's Customer Is

**Demographics:**
- Homeowner (not renter), single-family home in SW Virginia
- Age 35-65, skewing 45-60 for roof replacement decisions
- Household income $65K-$130K (middle to upper-middle class for the NRV/Roanoke market)
- Home value $200K-$450K, homes often 15-30 years old
- Mix of suburban (Christiansburg, Salem, Blacksburg) and semi-rural (Radford, Pulaski, Floyd)

**Psychographics:**
- Protective of their home as their biggest investment
- Value reliability and honesty over cheapest price
- Want to "do it right" — not cut corners
- Skeptical of contractors (burned before or heard horror stories)
- Research online before calling — reads reviews, checks credentials
- Prefers local companies they can hold accountable
- Many are dealing with storm damage and navigating insurance for the first time

### What They Fear

1. **Getting ripped off** — paying too much for subpar work
2. **Storm chasers** — fly-by-night crews who disappear after cashing the check
3. **Hidden costs** — the quote changes after work starts
4. **Voided warranties** — improper installation that costs them later
5. **Disruption** — how long will my house be torn up?
6. **Making the wrong choice** — this is a $10K-$25K decision they make once every 20 years

### What They Want

1. **Certainty** — "I chose the right company"
2. **Protection** — their family and investment are safe
3. **Transparency** — know exactly what they're paying for and why
4. **Proof** — see that others had great experiences
5. **Simplicity** — someone to guide them through the process (especially insurance)
6. **Value** — not cheapest, but worth every dollar

### Decision Triggers

- Roof is visibly aging (curling shingles, granule loss)
- Storm damage (hail, wind, fallen debris)
- Active leak or water damage
- Insurance company flagging roof age
- Neighbor got a new roof and it looks great
- Planning to sell and need curb appeal

## Part 3: Proposed Brand Voice

### Voice Summary: "The Trusted Expert Next Door"

MDR should sound like **the most knowledgeable roofer you've ever talked to who also happens to be your neighbor.** Not salesy. Not corporate. Not folksy-fake. Confident, direct, honest, and protective.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Confident, not arrogant** | We know we're good. We let credentials and results speak. | "We've earned GAF's highest honor — not by marketing, but by installing 600+ roofs the right way." |
| **Direct, not aggressive** | Say what needs saying. No fluff. No manipulation. | "Your roof has a lifespan. We'll tell you honestly where yours stands." |
| **Protective, not fear-mongering** | We care about your home. We don't scare you into buying. | "A small leak today becomes a $15,000 problem tomorrow. Let's catch it early." |
| **Local, not small-time** | We're your neighbors with world-class credentials. | "Two offices. One standard. Every roof in the NRV and Roanoke gets our full attention." |
| **Educational, not condescending** | We explain because informed homeowners make better decisions. | "Here's what actually happens during a roof replacement — no jargon, no surprises." |

### Words We Use
- Protect, safeguard, built to last
- Honest, transparent, straightforward
- Craftsmanship, precision, system (not just shingles)
- Your home, your family, your investment
- Inspect, assess, recommend (not "sell")

### Words We Avoid
- "Cheap," "discount," "deal" (we're premium)
- "Act now," "limited time," "don't miss out" (pressure tactics)
- "Trust us" (show, don't tell)
- "Best in the business" (generic)
- "Synergy," "leverage," "solutions" (corporate jargon)
- Exclamation marks in headlines (desperation)

### Tone Shifts by Context

| Page Type | Tone Dial | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Homepage | Confident + warm | First impression, build trust fast |
| Service pages | Expert + educational | They're researching, give substance |
| Area pages | Local + authoritative | "We know YOUR area" |
| About | Human + proud | Real people, real story |
| Contact/CTA | Direct + reassuring | Remove friction, reduce anxiety |
| Blog | Helpful + knowledgeable | Earn trust through value |
| Financing | Empathetic + practical | Money is stressful, be human |
| CRO popups | Respectful + valuable | Never manipulative |

## Part 4: Page-by-Page Copy Audit & Recommendations

### Homepage

**Current Hero:**
> "Virginia's Premier Roofing Contractor"
> "Serving Christiansburg, Roanoke & the New River Valley. Premium materials. Lifetime protection. 1,200+ roofs completed."

**Issues:**
- "Virginia's Premier Roofing Contractor" is generic — every roofer in Virginia says this
- Doesn't speak to the homeowner's problem or desire
- No emotional hook — purely credential-based

**Recommended Direction:**
> H1: "Your Roof Protects Everything That Matters."
> Sub: "GAF Master Elite certified. 600+ roofs across the NRV and Roanoke. The warranty, the craftsmanship, and the crew that shows up on time."
> CTA: "Get Your Free Inspection" / "Call (540) 553-6007"

**Why:** Leads with what the homeowner cares about (protection), then backs it with proof. Specificity ("shows up on time") signals real experience. 600 roofs is still very impressive — no need to inflate.

---

**Current Services Section:**
> "More Than Just A Roof."
> "We provide complete protection for your home's exterior, from the shingles to the gutters."

**Issues:**
- "More Than Just A Roof" is vague — doesn't tell them what MORE means
- Body copy is generic

**Recommended Direction:**
> H2: "Every Layer. Every Detail. Every Time."
> Sub: "A roof is a system — starter strips, underlayment, ventilation, shingles, ridge caps. We install the full GAF system so nothing gets missed."

**Why:** Educates while differentiating. Most roofers skip components. This signals thoroughness.

---

**Current Stats Counter:**
> "1,200+ Roofs Completed" / "15+ Years Serving Virginia" / "4.9 Avg. Google Rating" / "3x President's Club Winner"

**Issues:**
- **WRONG NUMBERS.** 1,200+ should be 600+. 15+ years should be 7 years (since 2019).
- Stats lack context — WHY should they care about President's Club?
- "3x President's Club Winner" means nothing to a homeowner

**Recommended Direction:**
> Correct stats with one-line context:
> "600+ Roofs Completed — and counting"
> "Since 2019 — same owners, same standards"
> "4.9 Google Rating — from homeowners like you"
> "GAF President's Club — awarded to the top 1% of contractors in the country"

---

**Current Financing CTA:**
> "Don't let budget concerns delay protecting your home."

**Issues:**
- "Budget concerns" feels like you're calling them broke
- Slightly condescending tone

**Recommended Direction:**
> "A new roof is a big investment. We make it easier with $0-down financing and payments that fit your monthly budget."

---

**Current Final CTA:**
> "Ready to Protect Your Home?"

**Issues:**
- Adequate but generic. Every roofer site ends with this.

**Recommended Direction:**
> "Let's See Where Your Roof Stands."
> "Book a free, no-pressure inspection. We'll give you an honest report — even if the answer is 'you're fine for now.'"

**Why:** The "even if you're fine" line is a massive trust builder. It signals honesty and removes the fear of being sold to.

---

### About Page

**Current Hero:**
> "Built on Integrity, Driven by Excellence"

**Issues:**
- Pure cliche. Could be any company in any industry.

**Recommended Direction:**
> "We Started With One Truck and One Promise."
> Sub: "Since 2019, Modern Day Roofing has grown from a small crew in Christiansburg to one of Virginia's most awarded roofing contractors. The promise hasn't changed: do the job right, every time, for every home."

**Note:** "Since 2019" is only 7 years — don't overstate longevity. Instead lean into SPEED of growth: "In just 7 years, we've become one of Virginia's most awarded roofing contractors" is actually more impressive than claiming 15 years.

---

**Current Values Cards:**
> "Integrity First" / "Award-Winning Quality" / "Locally Rooted"

**Issues:**
- Headlines are fine but body copy is generic
- "Our word is our bond" is cliche

**Recommended Direction:**
- "Integrity First" → "We'll Tell You the Truth" — "If your roof has 5 more years in it, we'll say so. We'd rather earn your trust now and your business later."
- "Award-Winning Quality" → "The Credentials to Back It Up" — "GAF doesn't hand out Master Elite status. We earned it by installing full roofing systems to manufacturer spec on every job."
- "Locally Rooted" → "Your Neighbors, Not a 1-800 Number" — "Two offices. Live in the community. When a storm hits the NRV, we're already here."

### CRO Popups — CRITICAL ISSUES

**Exit Intent Popup:**
> "$500 Off Your Roof Replacement"
> Dismiss: "No thanks, I'll pay full price"

**Issues:**
- **"No thanks, I'll pay full price" is a dark pattern.** This is manipulative and damages trust — the exact opposite of MDR's brand values. Shaming users into clicking is a tactic used by companies that don't respect their customers.
- $500 off — is this a real offer Alicia can honor?

**Resolution: $500 off and 0% financing are REAL offers (confirmed).** Fix the copy, keep the offers.

**Recommended Changes:**
> Dismiss button: "Not right now" (remove manipulative "I'll pay full price" text)
> Reframe headline: "Thinking about a new roof? Lock in $500 off with a free inspection this month."
> Keep $500 offer as-is, it's a strong hook

**Scroll Popup:**
> "0% Financing Available" — confirmed real offer, copy is fine
> Dismiss: "Maybe later" — already respectful, keep as-is

### Service Pages (Dynamic from CMS)

**Issues:**
- Headlines are just service names — functional but not compelling
- Missing: why MDR is better at THIS service than competitors
- Missing: process explanation (what happens step by step)
- Missing: FAQ per service

**Recommended Additions per Service Page:**
1. A "Why MDR for [Service]" section with 3 differentiators
2. A "What to Expect" process timeline
3. 2-3 FAQs specific to that service

---

### Area Pages (Dynamic from CMS)

**Issues:**
- Templated and thin — "Roofing Services in [City], VA" doesn't add value
- Google sees thin location pages as low-quality doorway pages

**Recommended Direction:**
- Add 1-2 paragraphs of genuinely local content per city (mention neighborhoods, common roof types, local weather patterns)
- Add "Recent projects in [City]" section linking to gallery
- Include local phone number if different for Roanoke vs Christiansburg office

---

### Google Reviews Section

**Issues:**
- Reviews are hardcoded, not pulling from Google
- 6 reviews all say essentially the same thing (professional, on time, great work)
- Missing variety — no insurance claim stories, no "they saved me money" stories

**Recommended:**
- Pull reviews dynamically if possible (or rotate a curated set of 12+)
- Select reviews that address different fears: insurance help, honest assessment, cleanup quality, warranty peace of mind

## Part 6: Brand Voice Skill (Phase 2 — After Approval)

Once Colin approves the voice direction, we build a `/mdr-content` skill that:

1. **Loads** the approved brand voice profile (voice attributes, ICP, words we use/avoid, tone dials)
2. **Accepts** a topic, keyword, or content brief
3. **Produces** blog posts, service page copy, or area page copy in MDR's voice
4. **Checks** against anti-patterns (no jargon, no dark patterns, no generic claims)
5. **Outputs** SEO-optimized, ICP-targeted content ready for Sanity CMS
