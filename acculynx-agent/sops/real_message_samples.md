## Patterns & Observations

### Automated Message Templates Identified

1. **Appointment Scheduled**, Triggered when appointment is created
   - Format: "Hello [Name], You appointment with [Rep] has been scheduled for [Date Time]. We look forward to seeing you then!"
   - Note: Contains typo "You appointment" (missing "r")

2. **Day-of Appointment Confirmation**, Sent morning of scheduled meeting
   - Format: "Hey [First Name], Please confirm our scheduled meeting today! [Rep Name] [Date Time] @ [Address]. [Phone]"

3. **1 Week Before Install**, Automated pre-install excitement message
   - Contains emojis (🎉🏠🔨)
   - Generic encouragement, mentions reaching out with questions

4. **1 Day Before Install**, Automated pre-install reminder
   - Contains emojis (🎉🏠🔨)
   - Includes rep phone AND project manager Wesley Diaz's number (540-494-1025)
   - More detailed contact info than 1-week version

### Rep Communication Styles

**Joe Furrow:**
- Direct and concise
- Post-install follow-up: "Hey [Name] this is Joe with Modern Day Roofing, I just wanted to reach out and see how everything went with the installation. Do you have any concerns?"
- Email follow-up: professional but brief, always includes phone number and "Reply directly to this email to respond"
- Internal notes: factual, action-oriented ("Getting him to sign today")

**Aric (AJ) Jaklitsch:**
- Warm, enthusiastic tone
- Uses exclamation points frequently
- Graceful handling of lost deals: "I appreciate you letting me know, Jason. If anything changes or you need a second opinion down the road, don't hesitate to reach out."
- Signs off as "AJ" in conversation

**Rodrigo Cordova (Production Coordinator):**
- Most verbose and detailed communicator
- Always signs messages "- Rodrigo"
- Proactive weather/scheduling updates
- Professional but personable tone
- Skilled at managing customer expectations during delays
- Uses urgency tactfully: "these spots tend to fill up quickly"
- Provides specific, actionable prep instructions

### Customer Response Patterns

- **Quick confirmers:** "See you soon" (Cliff), "Look forward to it!" (Bo), "Confirmed!" (Jason)
- **Engaged customers:** Bo Webster asked proactive questions about arrival time and prep needs
- **Polite decliners:** Jason Mullins was respectful when declining: "We decided to go with a different company. Thank you for the estimate though."
- **Reaction/Like messages:** Bo Webster used the "Like" reaction on messages (shows as "Liked '[message text]'")

### Key Sales Process Insights

1. **Lead → Appointment** is typically fast (1-2 days)
2. **Weather delays** are a major factor in Virginia roofing, Rodrigo sent 3 separate weather updates to Bo Webster over 4 weeks
3. **Customer patience management** is critical, Rodrigo's regular proactive updates kept Bo Webster engaged through a 2-month delay
4. **Scheduling flexibility** matters, Bo pushed back on the first offered date and Rodrigo smoothly accommodated
5. **Post-install follow-up** from the sales rep (Joe) focuses on satisfaction, not upselling
6. **Lost deal handling** (AJ with Jason) was professional and left the door open
