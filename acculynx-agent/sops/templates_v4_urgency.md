## URGENCY FRAMEWORK

**Real urgency triggers to use by season:**
- **April-September (Storm Season):** Active weather damage, saturated repair crews, material pricing pressure
- **October-November (Pre-Winter):** Last chance for dry installs before winter sets in, frozen ground complicates repairs
- **December-February:** Winter damage assessment season, ice dam risk, interior water damage compounds daily
- **March:** Post-winter damage often reveals worse conditions than visible from outside; spring install windows fill fast

**Social proof hierarchy:**
1. 231 five-star Google reviews (primary anchor)
2. GAF Master Elite / GAF certification (credibility for commercial, larger residential)
3. Years serving Christiansburg, Roanoke, and the New River Valley (local trust)
4. Post-install outcomes (real customer results)

**The cost-of-waiting principle:**
- A compromised roof gets worse, not better
- Water finds new paths every rain event
- Wood rot spreads. Mold follows
- Insurance carriers flag delayed claims
- Every week that passes is another week closer to the busy season

## USAGE NOTES

### Seasonal Urgency, Which Version to Use

| Month | Primary Urgency Angle |
|-------|----------------------|
| January-February | Winter damage is compounding; ice dams, interior leaks |
| March | Post-winter assessment; spring install windows fill fast |
| April-May | Storm season starting; get on schedule before demand peaks |
| June-August | Peak storm season; crews booking 3-5 weeks out |
| September | Last months of prime install weather; October transition |
| October-November | Closing window; hard to install in freezing temps |
| December | Emergency repair season; ice damage, insurance documentation |

### Social Proof Placement Rules

- Layer 1-3 (early trust): Mention 231 reviews + GAF as credibility signals
- Layer 4-5 (decision phase): Reference what reviews say about the experience (on time, no surprises, clean site)
- Layer 6 (last chance): Lead with 231 reviews as a hard trust signal, this is your closing argument
- Layer 8 (post-install): Ask for the review while referencing the 231 count, social proof begets more social proof
- Layer 9 (drip): Reference reviews organically; shift to referral value proposition
- Layer 11 (re-engagement): Brief mention, "still 231 reviews, still GAF certified", to signal nothing has degraded

### What Not to Do (Voice Standards Reminder)

- No emojis
- No all caps
- No fake deadlines ("offer expires Friday")
- No fake scarcity ("only 2 spots left this week")
- Never pressure around insurance timelines you cannot verify
- Never discuss competitor pricing or quality
- If a customer is angry: stop, escalate to COO immediately, do not attempt to de-escalate via template
