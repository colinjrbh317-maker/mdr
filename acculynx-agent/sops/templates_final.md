## Compilation Notes

### Win Count by Copywriter

| Version | Style | Wins |
|---------|-------|------|
| v5 | Conversational | 23 |
| v1 | Direct Response | 5 |
| v4 | Urgency + Social Proof | 1 |
| v2 | Relationship | 1 |
| v3 | Educator | 0 |

**v5 (Conversational) dominated** with 23 of 30 touchpoint wins. This is not surprising given the selection criteria heavily weighted "sounds like a real person texted this from their phone." v5 was written specifically to match real MDR rep voice samples, and it shows.

### Hardest Layers to Pick (Closest Competition)

1. **Layer 4, Touch 3 (Day 10 Email):** v1 vs. v5 was the tightest call. v5's conversational tone nearly won, but v1's bolded section headers (Financing / Warranty / Scheduling) made it more scannable for what is the single most important email in the entire pipeline. Scannability won.

2. **Layer 5, Touch 3 (Day 38 Email):** v4 vs. v5 was close. v5 had a cleaner voice, but v4's approach of quoting review themes rather than fabricating specific reviews was more credible and more compelling. v4 won on substance.

3. **Layer 6, Touch 1 (Day 52 Email):** v5 vs. v3 was competitive. v3's technical details (ice and water shield, drip edge, ventilation ratio) would impress a knowledgeable homeowner, but v5's "we don't use day-laborers" bullet hits harder for a broader audience and the overall tone was more appropriate for a going-cold email.

4. **Layer 10, Text vs. Email:** v1 and v2 split the channel wins here. v1's text was warmer; v2's email had the best single line ("this is your home and your decision").

### Recommended Hybrids

1. **Layer 3, Touch 1 (Post-Inspection Email):** Consider combining v5's conversational tone with v3's educational content about what makes estimates different (shingle grade, underlayment, decking). v5 wins for first deployment, but v3's educational angle could be A/B tested as a variant.

2. **Layer 9, Touch 4 (Day 365 Email):** v3's complimentary one-year inspection offer is a strong differentiator that v5 doesn't include. Consider adding one line to v5's version: "If you'd like us to do a complimentary one-year inspection, just let us know, no cost, no obligation."

3. **Layer 4, Touch 3 (Day 10 Email):** v3's GAF warranty tier breakdown (Silver / Gold / Golden Pledge) is genuinely useful content. Could be added as an optional attachment or linked resource rather than embedded in the email body.

### Overall Voice Consistency Assessment

The final template set has strong voice consistency because v5 won the vast majority of touches. The few non-v5 winners (v1 for Layer 4 Touch 3 email, Layer 7 Touch 3 text, Layer 9 Touches 1-2, Layer 10 text; v4 for Layer 5 Touch 3 email; v2 for Layer 10 email) are all slightly more formal in tone, which is appropriate since they correspond to either high-stakes moments (the day-10 estimate email), operational communications (pre-install reminder), post-close drip (longer relationship), or graceful exits (where formality signals respect).

The one area to watch: v1 and v2 use "Hi [Name]" while v5 uses "Hey [Name]." The final set mixes both, which is acceptable, "Hi" reads naturally in emails where formality is slightly higher, while "Hey" works in texts and conversational emails. No reader would notice the inconsistency across separate messages days or weeks apart.

**v3 (Educator) won zero touchpoints but produced the best supplemental content.** The Roofing Education Facts Bank and GAF warranty tier breakdown should be preserved as reference material for the AI agent to draw on during live conversations, even though the templated messages work better without that density.
