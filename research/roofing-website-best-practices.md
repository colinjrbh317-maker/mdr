## 1. Hero Section Best Practices

The hero section is the single most important conversion element on a roofing website. A visitor decides within 3-5 seconds whether to stay or leave. Every element must work in concert to establish credibility, communicate value, and drive action.

### 1.1 Full-Width Imagery

- **Use professional, full-width photography** of your actual crew working on a real project. Stock photography erodes trust immediately. If you must start with stock, replace it within 30 days.
- **Dark overlays (50-70% opacity)** on hero images ensure headline text remains legible across all screen sizes.
- **Video backgrounds** are increasingly popular among top-performing sites (e.g., Bone Dry Roofing uses branded video loops, Moss Roofing uses engaging hero video). Keep video files under 5MB or use a poster image with lazy-loaded video.
- **Dark themes are gaining significant traction** among roofing websites in 2025-2026. Darker backgrounds create dramatic contrast that helps your site stand out while making bright CTAs visually pop, potentially boosting click-through rates.
- **Avoid generic skyline or house shots.** Show workers on a roof, close-ups of quality materials, or an aerial drone shot of a completed project. These communicate competence, not just aesthetics.

### 1.2 Value-Driven Headlines

The headline must answer the visitor's core question: "Can you solve my problem?"

**High-performing headline formulas:**
- `[Benefit] + [Location]` -- "Trusted Roof Replacements in [City] Since [Year]"
- `[Pain Point] + [Solution]` -- "Storm Damage? Get a Free Inspection Within 24 Hours"
- `[Social Proof] + [Action]` -- "4.9-Star Rated | 2,000+ Roofs Installed | Get Your Free Estimate"

**Real examples from top-converting sites:**
- "Protect Your Most Important Investment" (value-driven, emotional)
- "$0 Down Until the Job is Done" (Cenvar Roofing -- risk-reversal)
- "America's Most Trusted Roofer" (Best Choice Roofing -- authority claim)
- "Your Roof. Our Reputation. Zero Compromises." (authority + commitment)

**Typography trend (2025-2026):** Bold, large fonts that dominate the hero section are on-trend, ensuring key information is easily digestible at a glance. Use clear, high-contrast type that works against both light and dark hero images.

**Subheadline:** Always pair the headline with a 1-2 sentence subheadline that adds specificity: service types, warranty details, or a trust signal ("Licensed, Insured & GAF Master Elite Certified").

### 1.3 CTA Placement: Form vs. Button

There are two dominant patterns for hero CTAs on high-converting roofing sites:

**Pattern A: Embedded Form (Higher conversion, higher commitment)**
- A short 3-4 field form embedded directly in the hero section
- Fields: Name, Phone, Email, Service Needed (dropdown)
- Best for: PPC landing pages where intent is high
- Conversion benchmark: 8-15% on paid traffic

**Pattern B: Primary Button (Lower friction, broader appeal)**
- A single bold CTA button: "Get My Free Estimate" or "Schedule Free Inspection"
- Links to a dedicated form page or triggers a modal
- Best for: Organic homepage traffic where visitors are still evaluating
- Conversion benchmark: 3-7% on organic traffic

**Recommendation:** Use Pattern B on the homepage hero with a secondary text link ("or call us now: (555) 123-4567"). Reserve Pattern A for dedicated landing pages tied to paid campaigns. CTA buttons should stand out with contrasting colors (orange, green, or red on dark backgrounds) and use verb-driven language: "Get Your Free Inspection," "Schedule Your Free Estimate."

**Key stat:** Using a specific, clear CTA can increase conversion rates by 161%. Personalized CTAs perform 202% better than basic CTAs.

### 1.4 Hero Section Checklist

- [ ] Full-width professional image or video of real work
- [ ] Headline under 10 words, benefit-driven, includes city if possible
- [ ] Subheadline with specifics (years in business, warranty, certifications)
- [ ] Primary CTA button with contrasting color
- [ ] Phone number visible (click-to-call on mobile)
- [ ] Trust indicator (star rating, review count, or certification badge)
- [ ] Page loads in under 2.5 seconds with hero image fully rendered (LCP target)

## 2. Trust Elements

Roofing is a high-ticket, low-frequency purchase. Homeowners are spending $8,000-$25,000+ and trusting a stranger on their roof. Trust elements are not optional -- they are conversion infrastructure.

### 2.1 Trust Bar (Below Hero)

Directly beneath the hero section, include a horizontal trust bar (auto-scrolling carousel on mobile). The best roofing website designs immediately bombard the visitor with proof of credibility: client testimonials, manufacturer certifications, badges, and association memberships.

**Tier 1 -- Manufacturer Certifications:**
- **GAF Master Elite** -- Only 2-3% of roofing contractors in North America qualify. This is the most recognizable certification in residential roofing. It unlocks the Golden Pledge Limited Warranty (50-year defect coverage + 25-year workmanship coverage). To qualify, a company must be licensed, maintain a satisfactory BBB rating verified by GAF, and commit to continuous crew training.
- **CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster** -- Demonstrates advanced installation training.
- **Owens Corning Preferred/Platinum Contractor** -- Access to enhanced warranty offerings. Best Choice Roofing highlights their Platinum status prominently.
- **TAMKO Pro Certified / Diamond Contractor** -- Regional relevance depending on market. Cenvar Roofing displays their TAMKO Diamond badge.

**Tier 2 -- Industry & Business Certifications:**
- **BBB A+ Accredited** -- Still one of the most recognized trust signals for homeowners over 40. The BBB verifies that accredited businesses meet their standards, including a commitment to resolve consumer complaints.
- **Angi Super Service Award** -- Validates customer satisfaction.
- **HomeAdvisor Elite Service / Top Rated** -- High visibility in lead-gen ecosystems.
- **GuildQuality** -- Skywalker Roofing displays their 99% recommendation rate through GuildQuality live satisfaction verification.
- **Google Guaranteed** -- Best Choice Roofing uses this badge effectively.

**Tier 3 -- Insurance & Licensing:**
- State contractor license number (displayed in footer and trust bar)
- "Fully Licensed & Insured" badge
- Workers' compensation verification

**Implementation:**
- Use an auto-scrolling horizontal carousel for the trust bar on mobile
- Display logos at consistent sizes (recommend 120px wide, grayscale with color on hover)
- Link each badge to its verification page when possible

**Key stat:** Trust signals boost on-site conversion rates by up to 33%, especially when displayed beside CTAs or near pricing sections.

### 2.2 Stats Counter Section

A stats counter section converts abstract credibility into concrete numbers. Place it on the homepage, typically after the hero or after the services overview.

**Recommended stats to display:**
| Stat | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Years in Business | "25+ Years" | Longevity = stability |
| Roofs Completed | "5,000+" | Volume = experience |
| Google Rating | "4.9 Stars" | Third-party validation |
| Review Count | "800+ Reviews" | Statistical significance |
| Warranty Coverage | "50-Year Warranty" | Risk reduction |

**Real examples:**
- Best Choice Roofing: "100,000+ grateful customers"
- Cenvar Roofing: "4.9 stars across 9,400+ reviews"
- Skywalker Roofing: "16,000+ finished projects"

**Implementation tip:** Use animated count-up on scroll-trigger (e.g., numbers animate from 0 to the final value as the section enters viewport). Libraries like CountUp.js work well. Keep the animation under 2 seconds.

### 2.3 Review Widgets

- **Embed live Google reviews** directly on the homepage using widgets that auto-sync (e.g., EmbedSocial, Elfsight, or Grade.us).
- Display the aggregate star rating prominently: "4.9/5 based on 847 Google Reviews."
- Show 3-4 individual reviews with the reviewer's name, date, and star rating.
- **Rotate reviews automatically** so fresh content appears without manual updates.
- Place review widgets near CTAs -- testimonials adjacent to "Get a Quote" buttons increase conversion by up to 34%.
- Cenvar Roofing aggregates reviews from Google, ConsumerAffairs, Angi, HomeAdvisor, and GuildQuality in one display.

**Key stat:** 84% of people trust an online review the same as a personal recommendation. Websites with social proof increase impressions by 25x compared to an average site.

## 3. Mobile-First Design

### 3.1 The Mobile Reality for Roofing

- **65-90% of roofing website traffic comes from mobile devices**, depending on the market and traffic source. PPC traffic skews even higher toward mobile.
- **80-90% of roofing searches now come from mobile** according to 2025-2026 local SEO studies.
- Homeowners often search from their driveway after a storm, standing next to damage. They need fast answers and immediate contact options.
- Google uses mobile-first indexing exclusively -- your mobile site IS your site for ranking purposes.
- Mobile-first design is no longer trendy -- it is survival. With over 60% of potential customers browsing on their phones (often while standing in their storm-damaged driveway), your site must work perfectly on small screens.

### 3.2 Touch Targets

- All tappable elements must be **minimum 44x44 pixels** (Apple HIG) or **48x48dp** (Material Design).
- Maintain **at least 8px spacing** between adjacent touch targets to prevent mis-taps.
- **Phone number links** must be large, prominent, and wrapped in `<a href="tel:+15551234567">` tags.
- Form input fields should be at least **44px tall** with generous padding.
- Use numeric keyboard triggers for phone number and ZIP code fields (`inputmode="tel"`, `inputmode="numeric"`).

### 3.3 Sticky CTAs

Sticky CTAs keep the conversion path visible at all times on mobile. They stay visible as users scroll, making it easy for mobile visitors to act anytime without hunting for the button.

**Sticky Mobile Bottom Bar (Recommended):**
```
+-------------------------------------------+
|  [Call Now]  |  [Free Estimate]            |
+-------------------------------------------+
```
- Fixed to the bottom of the viewport
- Two buttons: click-to-call (left) and form CTA (right)
- Use contrasting background color (e.g., brand primary or high-visibility orange)
- Height: 56-64px to be tappable without being intrusive
- Appears after the user scrolls past the hero section

**Data:** Optimizing CTAs on mobile increases conversion rates by up to 32.5%. Sticky or floating CTAs are perfect for mobile-first designs and action-focused experiences.

### 3.4 Click-to-Call

- **70% of roofing leads call directly** from ads and websites rather than filling out forms, making accurate phone tracking essential.
- The phone number must be clickable on every page, in the header, and in the sticky bar.
- Use `<a href="tel:+15551234567">` with tracking (CallRail, WhatConverts, or similar).
- Display the phone number as text (not an image) so it is accessible and crawlable.
- On desktop, the click-to-call can open a "call or text" prompt.
- Over 60% of local searches are conducted on mobile devices, with homeowners likely using their phones to find nearby contractors.

### 3.5 Mobile Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| Page Load (3G) | < 3 seconds | 53% of mobile users abandon after 3s; 40% abandon after 3s |
| LCP | < 2.5 seconds | Google Core Web Vital threshold |
| INP | < 200ms | Replaced FID as the responsiveness metric in 2024 |
| CLS | < 0.1 | Prevents layout shift frustration |
| Total Page Weight | < 2MB | Critical on slow mobile networks |

**Key stat:** A landing page that loads in under 2 seconds can convert up to 50% better than one that takes 5 seconds. Roofing contractors boost their lead volume by 40% just by shaving two seconds off their load time.

### 3.6 Mobile-Specific Design Patterns

- **Hamburger menu** with service categories as top-level items
- **Thumb-zone optimization:** Primary CTAs in the bottom 40% of the screen
- **Accordion FAQs** instead of long scrolling text blocks
- **Swipeable galleries** for project photos with pinch-to-zoom
- **Simplified forms** -- 3 fields max on mobile (Name, Phone, Service Type)
- **Single-column layout** for all forms
- **SMS/text option** as alternative to phone call
- **Maps integration** with "Get Directions" functionality

### 3.7 Mobile Performance Checklist

- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds on 4G connection
- [ ] All buttons and links are at least 48x48px
- [ ] Forms use appropriate mobile keyboard types
- [ ] Images are lazy-loaded and properly sized
- [ ] No horizontal scrolling on any page
- [ ] Click-to-call works on all phone numbers
- [ ] Google PageSpeed Insights score 90+ on mobile
- [ ] Content is readable without zooming
- [ ] Navigation is accessible with one hand
- [ ] Chat widget is mobile-friendly
- [ ] Sticky bottom bar with Call + Estimate buttons

## 4. Conversion Optimization

### 4.1 Exit-Intent Popups

Exit-intent popups detect when a desktop user's cursor moves toward the browser's close/back button and trigger a last-chance offer.

**2025 Performance Data:**
- Average popup conversion rate: **4.65%** (up from 4.01% in 2024, per Wisepops research)
- Cart abandonment exit popups: **17.12% average** (per OptiMonk data)
- Popups with countdown timers: **14.41% conversion** vs. 9.86% without (per OptiMonk)
- Bottom-center placement: **12.88% CVR** (dramatically outperforms top-right at 0.67%)
- Optimal display timing: **11-15 seconds** after page load for best UX + conversion balance

**Recommended exit-intent offers for roofing:**

**Offer 1: Free Inspection (Primary)**
```
----------------------------------------------
  Wait! Don't Leave Without Your
  FREE No-Obligation Roof Inspection

  [Name] [Phone] [ZIP Code]
  [Schedule My Free Inspection]

  "Limited appointments available this week"
----------------------------------------------
```

**Offer 2: Lead Magnet (Blog/Educational Pages)**
```
----------------------------------------------
  Before You Go...
  Download Our FREE Storm Damage Checklist

  [Enter Email] [Download Now]

  Plus: Get $250 off your next project
----------------------------------------------
```

**Offer 3: Storm Damage (Weather-Related Pages)**
```
----------------------------------------------
  Think Your Roof Has Storm Damage?
  Free Assessment -- We Handle the Insurance Paperwork

  [Schedule My Free Assessment]
----------------------------------------------
```

**On mobile:** Exit-intent does not work (no cursor). Instead, use **scroll-depth triggers** -- show the popup when the user scrolls back up after reaching 50%+ of the page.

**AI-powered personalization:** HubSpot data indicates personalized CTAs convert 42% more visitors compared to generic ones. AI-powered dynamic CTAs tailored to individual users can improve conversion rates by up to 44%.

### 4.2 Scroll-Triggered CTAs

- Trigger a slide-in CTA from the bottom-right corner when the user scrolls past 40-60% of the page.
- Keep it small and dismissible -- a mini card, not a full overlay.
- Message: "Need a quick answer? Call us now or chat with our team."
- This works on both desktop and mobile.

### 4.3 Sticky Mobile Bar

As detailed in Section 3.3, the sticky bottom bar is the single highest-impact mobile conversion element. Every roofing site should implement it.

### 4.4 Form Optimization

**Single-Step Forms (for simple lead capture):**
- 3-4 fields maximum: Name, Phone, Email, Service Type (dropdown)
- Conversion rate benchmark: 4-7%

**Multi-Step Forms (for higher-quality leads):**
Multi-step forms with micro-commitment steps can increase engagement by 2-3x and produce 100-300% more signups than single-step forms. Micro-commitment increased interactions by 8-9x in testing.

**Recommended multi-step flow for roofing:**

| Step | Fields | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Step 1 | "What service do you need?" (visual selector: Repair / Replacement / Inspection / Storm Damage) | Low-friction entry, micro-commitment |
| Step 2 | "What type of roof?" (Asphalt Shingle / Metal / Tile / Flat / Not Sure) | Qualifies the lead |
| Step 3 | "When do you need service?" (ASAP / This Week / This Month / Just Researching) | Prioritization signal |
| Step 4 | Name, Phone, Email, Address | Contact capture after commitment |

**Design tips:**
- Show a progress bar ("Step 2 of 4")
- Use large, tappable icon-based selectors instead of radio buttons on mobile
- Auto-advance to the next step on selection (no "Next" button needed for single-choice steps)
- Final step: "Your free estimate is almost ready!" with a submit button

**Conversion benchmarks:**
- Average landing page conversion rate: 4.3%
- Top 25% of landing pages convert at 11.5% or more
- Top-performing roofing landing pages see conversion rates of 10%+
- A conversion rate around 5% is considered strong for roofing service websites

### 4.5 Speed-to-Lead

- **Over 40% of roofing leads go to the first contractor to respond.**
- Integrate form submissions with instant SMS/email notifications to the sales team.
- Auto-responder text within 60 seconds: "Thanks [Name]! A roofing specialist will call you within 5 minutes."
- Use tools like Podium, ServiceTitan, Hatch, or JobNimbus for automated speed-to-lead workflows.

### 4.6 A/B Testing Priorities

Test these elements in order of likely impact:
1. CTA button text ("Get Free Estimate" vs. "Schedule Free Inspection" vs. "Get My Quote")
2. Hero headline copy
3. Form length (3 fields vs. 4 fields)
4. Exit-intent offer type
5. Trust bar placement (below hero vs. within hero)
6. Phone number visibility in header

## 5. Service Page Structure

Every major service offered deserves its own dedicated page. This is critical for both SEO and conversion. A single "Services" page that lists everything underperforms compared to individual, deeply optimized service pages. Each service and service area should have a specific and separate page.

### 5.1 Core Service Pages to Build

| Page | Target Keyword | Priority |
|------|---------------|----------|
| Roof Replacement | "roof replacement [city]" | High |
| Roof Repair | "roof repair [city]" | High |
| Storm Damage Repair | "storm damage roof repair [city]" | High |
| Roof Inspection | "roof inspection [city]" | Medium |
| New Roof Installation | "new roof installation [city]" | Medium |
| Emergency Roofing | "emergency roof repair [city]" | Medium |
| Metal Roofing | "metal roofing [city]" | Medium |
| Flat Roofing / Commercial | "commercial roofing [city]" | Low-Medium |
| Gutter Installation | "gutter installation [city]" | Low |
| Siding | "siding contractor [city]" | Low |
| Solar Roofing | "solar roofing [city]" | Low (emerging) |

### 5.2 Service Page Template

Each service page should follow this structure:

```
1. H1: [Service Name] in [Primary City], [State]
   Example: "Expert Roof Replacement in Austin, TX"

2. Hero Image: Project photo relevant to this specific service

3. Opening Paragraph (150-200 words):
   - What the service is and who it is for
   - Why the homeowner needs it now
   - Include primary keyword naturally

4. "Get a Free Estimate" CTA Button

5. H2: Why Choose [Company] for [Service]?
   - 3-4 bullet points: experience, certifications, warranty, local reputation
   - Inline trust badges

6. H2: Our [Service] Process
   - Step-by-step numbered list (Inspection > Proposal > Scheduling > Installation > Final Walkthrough)
   - This reduces anxiety by making the process predictable
   - Visual timeline or flowchart (like Best Choice Roofing's 4-step process)

7. H2: [Service] Cost in [City]
   - Price ranges or "factors that affect cost" (do NOT list exact prices unless that is your strategy)
   - Note: CFL Roofing is notable for being one of the only companies that puts price tags directly on CTAs
   - Link to financing page
   - CTA: "Get Your Personalized Quote"

8. H2: Before & After Gallery
   - 3-6 project photos specific to this service type
   - Include brief captions with location and scope

9. H2: What Our Customers Say
   - 2-3 testimonials from customers who received this specific service
   - Include name, city, and star rating

10. H2: Frequently Asked Questions
    - 5-8 FAQs specific to this service
    - Use FAQPage schema markup
    - Example: "How long does a roof replacement take?" / "Will my insurance cover storm damage repair?"

11. H2: Service Areas
    - List of cities/neighborhoods served
    - Link to individual location pages

12. Final CTA Section
    - "Ready to Get Started? Schedule Your Free [Service] Estimate Today"
    - Phone number + form button
```

### 5.3 On-Page SEO for Service Pages

- **Title tag:** Under 60 characters. Format: `[Service] in [City], [State] | [Company Name]`
- **Meta description:** Under 160 characters. Include CTA language and value statement: "Free estimates. Licensed & insured. Call today."
- **URL structure:** `/services/roof-replacement/` or `/roof-replacement-[city]/`
- **H1:** Focused heading that reflects user intent, with supporting H2s and H3s that guide readers through the content.
- **Internal linking:** Link to related services, location pages, and relevant blog posts.
- **Image alt text:** Descriptive and keyword-rich: "roof replacement project in north Austin showing new architectural shingles"
- **Content depth:** Each service page should have 800-1,500 words of unique content.

## 6. Location Pages

Location pages are essential for roofing companies serving multiple cities or metro areas. They target "[service] in [city]" searches, which represent the highest-intent traffic.

### 6.1 Why Location Pages Matter

- "Roof repair near me" and "[city] roofing contractor" are among the most valuable keywords in the roofing vertical.
- 80-90% of roofing searches now come from mobile devices, most with local intent.
- Google rewards genuinely localized content with higher rankings in the Map Pack and organic results.
- Building dedicated location pages for each service area helps increase local search traffic in those areas.
- With dedicated location pages, you can target keywords like "roofing services in [City A]" on one page and "roofing services in [City B]" on the other, attracting highly relevant traffic from each location.

### 6.2 Location Page Template

```
URL: /service-areas/[city-name]/
Example: /service-areas/round-rock-tx/

1. H1: Roofing Services in [City], [State]
   "Trusted Roofing Contractor in Round Rock, TX"

2. Hero Section:
   - Background image: Completed project IN that city (critical for authenticity)
   - City name prominently displayed
   - CTA: "Get a Free Estimate in [City]"

3. Opening Content (200-300 words):
   - Mention specific neighborhoods, landmarks, or local context
   - Reference local weather patterns and how they impact roofs
   - Example: "Round Rock homeowners face unique roofing challenges
     from Central Texas hail storms, particularly during spring
     severe weather season..."

4. H2: Our Roofing Services in [City]
   - Grid of service cards linking to individual service pages
   - Tailor descriptions to local relevance

5. H2: Recent Projects in [City]
   - 3-4 project photos FROM that specific city
   - Include street-level detail: "This homeowner on [Street/Neighborhood]
     needed..."
   - This is the most important section for avoiding Google's doorway
     page penalty

6. H2: What [City] Homeowners Say About Us
   - Reviews from customers specifically in that city
   - Embed Google reviews filtered by location if possible

7. H2: Why [City] Homeowners Choose [Company]
   - Local response time: "We're just [X] minutes away in [nearby HQ]"
   - Local code compliance knowledge
   - HOA experience in local neighborhoods

8. H2: [City] Roofing FAQ
   - City-specific questions:
     "What roofing materials work best for [City]'s climate?"
     "Do I need a permit for roof replacement in [City]?"
     "How long does a typical roof last in [region]?"

9. Embedded Google Map
   - Showing your business location relative to this city
   - Reinforces the local connection

10. Service Areas Near [City]
    - Links to neighboring location pages
    - Helps with internal linking structure

11. Final CTA + NAP
    - Consistent Name, Address, Phone matching Google Business Profile
```

### 6.3 Avoiding Doorway Page Penalties

Google specifically penalizes thin, duplicate location pages. To stay safe:

- **Every location page must have unique content** -- at minimum 60% unique text.
- **Include real projects, real reviews, and real local details** from that specific city.
- **Do NOT just swap city names** in a template and publish 50 identical pages.
- **Show jobs completed in that city** with photos, customer names (with permission), and specific project details.
- **Include city-specific FAQs** that reference local regulations, climate, and common issues.
- **Link naturally** from the main navigation or a service areas hub page.
- A shallow URL structure and clear parent-child hierarchy let Google recrawl fresh pages without blowing its budget, and every service-city URL should be linked from the main navigation or XML sitemap.

### 6.4 Local SEO Signals Per Location Page

- Add `LocalBusiness` schema with that city's details (add a separate LocalBusiness schema block for each location page if operating in several cities)
- Include a unique meta description referencing the city
- Use location-specific keywords: "roof repair in [city name]" or "residential roofing services near [area]" in title tags, meta descriptions, headers, and throughout content
- Add driving directions or "Areas we serve near [City]" with surrounding neighborhoods
- Ensure NAP consistency across the page, Google Business Profile, and all citations
- Embed Google Map of the service area

### 6.5 Google Business Profile Optimization (Per Location)

Your GBP is your most valuable local asset:

- **Primary category:** "Roofing Contractor"
- **Service areas:** Define accurately for each location
- **Roofing services:** Add each service individually
- **Photos:** Upload weekly -- project photos, team photos, completed jobs
- **Reviews:** Actively solicit and respond to all reviews (Google factors in response rate and recency)
- **Posts:** Weekly Google Business posts with offers, updates, and project showcases

**Key stat:** 90% of homeowners use Google before choosing a roofer. Top 3 Google Maps results capture over 70% of calls.

## 7. Social Proof

84% of people trust an online review as much as a personal recommendation. For roofing -- a high-anxiety, high-cost purchase -- social proof is the deciding factor between you and the next contractor.

### 7.1 Before-and-After Galleries

**Implementation best practices:**
- Use **interactive slider comparisons** (drag handle reveals before/after). Recommended tools:
  - **BEAF plugin** (WordPress): Unlimited sliders and galleries, supports horizontal/vertical, fully responsive, touch-compatible on mobile, lightweight with WebP support
  - **WP Before After Image Slider** (WordPress): Built for professionals who need visual impact
  - **SeedProd Before After Toggle** (WordPress): Side-by-side with horizontal/vertical layouts
  - **Elfsight Before and After Slider**: Works on any platform, unlimited comparison sets
  - **img-comparison-slider** (custom/React builds): Lightweight JavaScript library
- **Both images must be the same dimensions** (same angle, same framing, same pixel width and height) for the slider to work properly.
- Organize galleries **by service type** (Roof Replacement, Storm Damage, Metal Roofing) and by **roof type** (Asphalt Shingle, Tile, Metal, Flat).
- Include captions with: city/neighborhood, scope of work, materials used, and timeline.
- **Show the ugly truth** -- dramatic before photos of damaged roofs convert better than subtle differences.

**Gallery page structure:**
- Filterable grid layout (filter by service type, material, city)
- Each project card shows: before/after thumbnail, city, service type
- Click to expand: full slider, project description, testimonial from that customer
- Add `ImageObject` schema to project photos

**Key stat:** Websites with professional project galleries convert up to 30% better than those with poor-quality images or no gallery.

### 7.2 Video Testimonials

Video testimonials are significantly more persuasive than text reviews because they show body language, emotion, and authenticity. While written reviews are helpful, video testimonials are more authentic and powerful as they show the lived experience of the customer.

**Production guidelines:**
- **Length:** 60-90 seconds (2-3 minutes maximum)
- **Format:** Customer standing in front of their completed roof
- **Structure:** Problem > Discovery > Experience > Result > Recommendation
- **Quality:** Smartphone video is fine if audio is clear. Use a lapel mic ($20-30 investment).
- **Quantity:** Aim for 3-5 video testimonials minimum on the site.

**Placement:**
- Embed 1-2 videos on the homepage (below the fold, near the CTA section)
- Place service-specific video testimonials on corresponding service pages
- Create a dedicated "Customer Stories" or "Testimonials" page
- Upload to YouTube with optimized titles for additional SEO value

**Real examples:**
- Best Choice Roofing: Video testimonial carousel with automatic rotation
- Skywalker Roofing: YouTube content including real roof installation demonstrations and founder story video
- Cenvar Roofing: Customer video testimonials on the homepage

### 7.3 Google Review Integration

- **Display aggregate rating prominently:** "4.9/5.0 from 847 Google Reviews" in the hero or trust bar.
- **Auto-sync widget:** Use EmbedSocial, Elfsight, Grade.us, or Birdeye to pull reviews automatically. Fresh reviews appear on your site without manual updates, providing fresh content every time a new review posts.
- **Star rating schema:** Implement `AggregateRating` schema on the homepage to display stars in Google search results.

### 8.4 Core Web Vitals

These are Google's page experience signals. Failing them means ranking below competitors who pass. Interaction to Next Paint (INP) replaced First Input Delay (FID) as the decisive metric in 2024, and any INP above 200ms now drags pages below faster-loading rivals.

| Metric | Threshold | How to Achieve |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint) | < 2.5s | Optimize hero image (WebP/AVIF, preload with `fetchpriority="high"`, proper sizing), use CDN, server-side rendering |
| **INP** (Interaction to Next Paint) | < 200ms | Minimize JavaScript, defer non-critical scripts, avoid long tasks, keep under 200ms across both 4G and Wi-Fi |
| **CLS** (Cumulative Layout Shift) | < 0.1 | Set explicit `width`/`height` on images and embeds, avoid dynamic content injection above the fold |

**Image optimization specifics:**
- Convert all images to **WebP or AVIF** format (50-65% smaller than JPEG with equivalent quality).
- Use `loading="lazy"` on all images EXCEPT the hero/LCP image. Never lazy load the LCP element -- this is a common mistake that hurts pagespeed.
- Use `fetchpriority="high"` on the hero image. Consider `<link rel="preload">` for the hero image.
- Set explicit `width` and `height` attributes on all `<img>` tags to prevent CLS.
- Serve responsive images with `srcset` for different viewport widths.

**Performance tools:**
- Google PageSpeed Insights (lab + field data)
- Chrome DevTools Lighthouse
- WebPageTest.org (waterfall analysis)
- CrUX Dashboard (real user data)
- NitroPack (automated optimization for WordPress -- used by 250,000+ sites)

**Audit cadence:** Re-crawl priority pages monthly. Quarterly audits of Core Web Vitals, content freshness, and backlink quality ensure rankings stay resilient through algorithm updates.

## 12. ADA Compliance

### 12.1 The Legal Landscape (2025-2026)

- ADA website lawsuits surged **37% year-over-year** in the first half of 2025, with **2,014 federal lawsuits** filed in H1 2025 alone (concentrated in New York, California, and Florida).
- **64% of lawsuits targeted companies with annual revenues under $25 million** -- this includes most roofing companies.
- Typical settlements range from **$5,000 to $50,000**, plus attorney fees and remediation costs.
- **WCAG 2.1 Level AA** is the de facto compliance standard referenced by courts and the DOJ.
- WCAG 2.2 (published October 2023) is the current version with 86 testable success criteria across three conformance levels.
- The guidelines are organized around four principles: content must be **Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust (POUR).**

### 12.2 Critical Warning: Accessibility Overlays Do NOT Work

- **456 lawsuits in H1 2025 (22.6% of all filings)** targeted websites that had accessibility overlay widgets installed. Overlays demonstrably fail to protect from litigation and can increase risk.
- In 2025, the FTC reached a **$1 million settlement with accessiBe** for misleading businesses by marketing its widget as a guaranteed compliance tool. The order prohibits representations that automated tools -- including AI-driven widgets -- can make any website WCAG compliant or ensure continued automatic compliance.
- Installing an overlay signals awareness of accessibility obligations. If your site remains non-compliant despite the overlay, plaintiffs can argue you knew about the problem and chose a cheap fix over real remediation.
- **The only reliable defense is structural, code-level compliance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA.**

### 12.3 WCAG Compliance Checklist for Roofing Websites

**Perceivable:**
- [ ] All images have descriptive `alt` text (not just "image1.jpg" -- describe what the image shows)
- [ ] Videos have captions/subtitles
- [ ] Color contrast ratio is at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text
- [ ] Information is not conveyed by color alone (e.g., form errors must include text, not just red highlighting)
- [ ] Text can be resized to 200% without loss of content or functionality

**Operable:**
- [ ] All interactive elements are keyboard-accessible (Tab, Enter, Space, Arrow keys)
- [ ] Focus indicators are visible on all focusable elements
- [ ] No keyboard traps (user can always Tab away from any element)
- [ ] Skip navigation link at the top of the page ("Skip to main content")
- [ ] Touch targets are at least 44x44 pixels
- [ ] No content that flashes more than 3 times per second

**Understandable:**
- [ ] Page language is declared (`<html lang="en">`)
- [ ] Form fields have visible labels (not just placeholder text)
- [ ] Error messages are clear and specific ("Please enter a valid phone number" not just "Error")
- [ ] Navigation is consistent across all pages

**Robust:**
- [ ] HTML is valid and well-formed
- [ ] ARIA roles and attributes are used correctly
- [ ] Content works with screen readers (test with NVDA or VoiceOver)
- [ ] Site functions in all major browsers and assistive technologies

### 12.4 Practical Steps for Roofing Websites

1. **Audit first.** Use axe DevTools (free browser extension), WAVE, or Lighthouse accessibility audit. Identify all issues.
2. **Fix the highest-impact items first:** alt text, color contrast, keyboard navigation, form labels.
3. **Test with real assistive technology.** Turn on VoiceOver (Mac/iOS) or NVDA (Windows) and try to complete a form submission using only the keyboard + screen reader.
4. **Publish an accessibility statement** at `/accessibility/` declaring your commitment and providing a contact method for users who encounter barriers.
5. **Conduct a professional audit annually** or after major site redesigns. Services like Accessible.org, UsableNet, or Level Access provide WCAG audits.
6. **Remediate continuously.** Accessibility is not a one-time project. Every new page, feature, or widget must be checked.

### 12.5 Common Roofing Website Accessibility Failures

| Issue | Where It Appears | Fix |
|-------|-----------------|-----|
| Missing alt text | Project gallery photos | Add descriptive alt text to every image |
| Poor contrast | Light gray text on white background | Use contrast ratio tool; ensure 4.5:1 minimum |
| Inaccessible forms | Quote request forms | Add `<label>` elements tied to inputs with `for`/`id` |
| No keyboard navigation | Image sliders, dropdown menus | Ensure all interactive elements receive focus |
| Auto-playing video | Hero section video | Provide pause/stop control, no autoplay with audio |
| Missing page titles | Service and location pages | Every page needs a unique, descriptive `<title>` |
| Chat widget inaccessible | Chatbot overlay | Choose a chatbot vendor that is WCAG compliant |
| Placeholder-only labels | Contact forms | Use visible `<label>` elements, not just placeholders |
